A volunteer dog walker is a person who voluntarily offers their time and services to walk dogs. The goal of a volunteer dog walker is to provide the dogs with exercise, socialization, and human interaction to improve their physical and mental well-being while they wait to be adopted.

Something about our
dog walkers

A volunteer dog sitter is a person who voluntarily offers their time and services to look after dogs temporarily, usually in the owner's home. They may be responsible for feeding, walking, and providing companionship for the dog(s) while the owner is away. The goal of a volunteer dog sitter is to provide a loving, safe and comfortable environment for the dogs, keeping the dogs company and making sure they are well taken care of while the owner is away.

A dog trainer is a professional who specializes in teaching dogs obedience, behavior modification and other skills. They use positive reinforcement techniques to help dogs learn and develop good manners, and help pet owners address specific issues such as excessive barking, aggression, or separation anxiety. Dog trainers work with a variety of dogs and their owners, from puppies to adult dogs and from pet dog to working dogs. Dog trainers can work independently, or as part of a training school or pet store. They can also work with dogs in different settings such as homes, parks, or training centers. The goal of a dog trainer is to help dogs and their owners develop a strong bond and to help dogs become well-behaved and well-adjusted members of society.
